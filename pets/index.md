## Dogs - Henri & Eevi

### Feeding

The dogs are fed in their crates. You can tell them to go into their crates and ask if they want “Breakfast” or “Dinner”, they should go right in. They both get a scoop of food. I usually give Henri a generous portion. Henri gets the slow feeder and Eevi gets the normal bowl.

### Walking
They both enjoy going on walks, although sometimes Henri doesn’t want to and that’s okay. Their leashes are in the basket in the cube organizer next to the piano. Eevi’s has a harness on it, Henri’s does not. Just be careful: **Henri can slip out of his collar.**

### Water
Their water is next to the counter in the kitchen. Please just keep an eye on it make sure that it has water in it.

### Outside
Feel free to let the dogs out whenever. Just try to get Eevi to come inside if she is barking too much. We try not to bother the neighbors. Sometimes if it is rainy their paws might get muddy. We leave a towel on the chair by the back door that we wipe them off with. Its best to do this one at a time. **Henri can get weirdly aggressive when Eevi gets her paws wiped off, not sure why.** If they get really dirty you might have to rinse their paws off in the bathtub, and its best to do this separately too so Henri doesn’t get upset.

### Henri
* Henri has some aggression issues. He is protective over food and high value treats which is why we feed the dogs in their crates. If you are doing regular treats and giving one to each dog he is usually fine. 

* He is also afraid of thunderstorms and fireworks. If you are aware that a storm is coming you should give him half of a trazodone pill (in the left cabinet in the laundry room) in a pill pocket, and give Eevi an empty pill pocket. It takes about an hour or so to kick in.

* He also has a broken tooth in the front which probably hurts. He is scheduled for a surgery to get it out, but in the meantime we think the pain makes him more irritable. He has gotten upset at the cats a few times lately, which he normally doesn’t do. Ollie and Andy stay away from him for the most part but Freya is still learning so it is best to not let her get too close to Henri right now.

### Eevi

Eevi’s breed was bred to herd reindeer and they do that by barking at them. So needless to say Eevi barks a lot. We are trying to train her to not do it as much. The main thing is to not give her attention when she is barking. She will bark when she wants to be let out of her crate. We will try to avoid this by not moving toward her or looking at her until she stops barking. She has kind of started to figure it out so she will kind of stop. Its kind of like a red-light green-light game with her. If you could try to do this as well it would be really helpful.
Eevi is also not fixed (we are planning on fixing her when she is old enough). However she just came out of heat and female dogs only usually go into heat twice a year, so you shouldn’t have to worry about it. 

### Sleeping
We usually let the dogs sleep on the bed with us, so that is probably what they would prefer but if you are not comfortable with that I am sure that they would sleep in their crates. Eevi might whine a bit at first though. If they do sleep with you its best to keep the door shut so Eevi doesn’t get into anything while you are sleeping. We learned recently that she can fit through the hole on top of the kitty litter box cabinet. So she will try to get in there to eat kitty poops if unattended. Also it helps to keep the cats out at night. Oliver likes to lick during the night and we find it really annoying.
